02

Each particle that populates the screen has its own magnitude of attraction and repulsion towards each other. The magnitude of force for individual particles vary depending on various factors like distance of the particle from each other ( force of attraction increased with the increase in distance) and scale (size of the particle was directly proportional to the magnitude of the internal force).


The pattern was a result of the traces left behind by the particle in constant motion navigating its way to find new relationships.

The direction of rotation varies with the change in relationship between the particles. If the particle senses a larger force of attraction over repulsion, it rotates clockwise and vice versa. The intensity of the force built in the particle directs the course of its journey. Which resonated so much with life to me.

If ( Attractive Force > Repulsive Force) : Clockwise rotation

else : Anti-clockwise rotation

Circle[] c=new Circle[100];
PVector centre;
void setup()
{
  size(600,600,P3D);
 background(255);
 for(int i=0;i<100;i++)
{
  centre=new PVector(0,0);
 c[i]=new Circle(centre); }
}
void draw()
{
 translate(width/2,height/2);
 for(int i=0;i<100;i++)
  {
  //c[i].Circle();
  c[i].attraction();
  c[i].centrifugal();
  c[i].check();
  c[i].update();
  c[i].show();
} }
