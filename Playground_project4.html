Message in a bottle

Interactive Product design

A bottle that traps the sounds of nature from 15 disappearing lakes in the city of Bangalore(India) to raise awareness regarding water pollution

IDEA

01

The inspiration for this project came from a discussion with a Herpatologist Mr. Shiva kumar. He shared that the frogs in Bangalore City were at high risk.
Until I spoke to him, I had never observed any frogs in Bangalore. This made me think that the city has grown so much that urbanization has subdued the voice of all the flora and fauna to whome the city belonged to, as much as it did to us. So I decided to start looking at the lakes of Bangalore.
As the city has always been referred to as

“ The Land of 1000 lakes ”

PROCESS

02

In the current age of environmental issues, the changed perception of people towards nature as a mere commodity has been the driving factor to create this project.
Looking at the potential of interactive technologies as a medium to bridge the gap in communication between man and his environment. The direction adopted could be a tangible interface or other tools for utilizing data to better our understanding of our role in the whole ecosystem.

Flow of Interaction

SOUNDS

03

The bottle that belonged to each lake had a distinct combination of sounds that belonged to the creatures that dwelled in the lake bed. The sounds were recorded using my phone and cleaned later using Audacity software.

The bottle held the rare sounds of birds , insects and frogs that had been subdued and over powered by the noise of our chaotic world.

DESIGN INTENT

04

The project aims to bring together conservationists, researchers, communities, government, young and old to work towards saving these lakes that are dying due to the current actions of man.
It is a strife to make the society more empathetic towards nature. Even the smallest gesture of not consciously throwing garbage into the lake, would be a successfully delivered message.
